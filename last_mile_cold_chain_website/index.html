Last-Mile Cold Chain System for Vaccines

Team Enneagon — Portable solar-powered solution for remote regions

Download Project PDF

Problem Statement

Vaccines lose potency outside the 2–8°C range. Remote regions lack reliable electricity which leads to cold chain failures and high wastage. Our solution focuses on affordability, portability, and durability.

Objectives

  • Maintain temperature constantly (2–8°C)
  • Operate independently of grid electricity
  • Provide a reliable, sustainable vaccine storage

System Design (Summary)

The design uses solar panels, thermal energy storage (PCM packs), vacuum insulated box and an ESP32 for monitoring (Wi‑Fi/Bluetooth). See page images and diagram in the PDF for sketches and layout (refer to the system design on page 6 of the PDF).

Advantages & Impact

  • Sustainability via solar power
  • Reliability during outages
  • Low operating cost and scalability
  • Improved vaccine safety — reduced spoilage

SDG Contributions

Contributes to SDG 3 (Good Health), SDG 9 (Industry & Innovation), and SDG 13 (Climate Action). For details, see the PDF (pages 7-9).
